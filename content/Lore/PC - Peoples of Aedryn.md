## Humans

Humans are the most numerous people within Aedryn and can be found in every region of the continent. While particularly associated with Velmora, human settlements exist throughout the world and encompass an enormous range of cultures, beliefs and professions.

Their adaptability and ambition have allowed them to thrive in almost every environment, making them one of the most influential peoples of the modern age.



## Elves

Elves are most commonly associated with the Verdant Expanse and the Ellei. Known for their long lives and deep connection to history, many elves view themselves as caretakers of traditions that predate modern civilisation.

While often perceived as reserved by outsiders, elven communities possess a strong sense of identity and responsibility towards the natural world.



## Vanara

Vanara are Monkey Folk  native to the Verdant Expanse and form a significant portion of the Ellei. Curious, energetic and highly social, they are often more willing than their elven counterparts to engage with the wider world.

Despite their reputation for mischief, many are accomplished scholars, scouts and diplomats.



## Ratfolk

The Ratfolk of Aedryn are renowned for their ingenuity, adaptability and thirst for knowledge. Once forced to survive within the shadows of the Dwarven Empire, they now form the backbone of the Redentan and are responsible for many of the technological advancements emerging from the Stoneward Spine.

Many view the world as a puzzle waiting to be understood.



## Orcs

Orcs played a significant role in the fall of the Dwarven Empire and today form an important part of the Folkmoor Creed. Their history is marked by resilience, perseverance and a determination never to be controlled again.

Contrary to many old stereotypes, modern orc communities are as diverse as any other people of Aedryn.


## Automatons

Created by the Dwarven Empire, automatons were originally built to serve as labourers and soldiers. Their rebellion during the Smoke War became one of the defining moments of modern history.

Today, automatons can be found throughout Aedryn, pursuing lives of their own choosing and continuing to define what freedom means for a people who were once considered property.
