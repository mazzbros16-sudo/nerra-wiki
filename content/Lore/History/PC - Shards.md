## At A Glance

The Shards are the great landmasses of Nerra and serve as the closest equivalent to continents in the modern world. Each Shard is separated from the others by the vast voids known as the Sunders, making travel between them difficult, expensive and often dangerous.

Most scholars believe the Shards were formed during the aftermath of the Cataclysm, when the world fractured into the divided form known today. Whether the Shards were once connected as a single landmass remains one of the most widely accepted theories regarding the ancient world.

Five major Shards dominate modern maps: Thyren, Kareth, Caelyx, Morvane and Brassreach. Each possesses its own cultures, political powers, histories and dangers. While trade and communication exist between them, most people will spend their entire lives without ever setting foot upon another Shard.

To many, the Shards represent more than geography. They are a reminder that the world was once something greater, and perhaps something far stranger, than it appears today.

## Historical Account

_Excerpt from **The Divided World: A Survey of the Great Shards of Nerra**, authored by Cartographer-Magister Elric Thorne of the Royal Geographic Society of Velor._

---

To understand modern Nerra, one must first understand the Shards.

Most travellers spend their lives concerned with kingdoms, cities and borders. These things are certainly important, but they are ultimately temporary. Governments rise and fall. Empires expand and collapse. Roads appear and disappear beneath the passage of centuries.

The Shards endure.

No matter where one travels within the known world, the presence of the Shards shapes every aspect of life. Trade routes, political alliances, migration, religion and warfare all exist within the constraints imposed by these colossal divisions of land.

What exactly a Shard is remains a surprisingly difficult question to answer.

The common description is that they are continents separated by the Sunders. While broadly accurate, this explanation ignores the unusual nature of their formation. Viewed from above, many Shards resemble immense craters or basins, surrounded by towering stone walls that rise above the lands contained within them. Some scholars argue this is evidence of catastrophic geological upheaval. Others suggest the very laws governing the world were altered during the Cataclysm.

As with so many subjects relating to that ancient disaster, certainty remains elusive.

Of the known Shards, Thyren is the largest and most influential. It is home to the continents of Aedryn and Evellon and serves as the political and economic heart of much of modern civilisation. Most major powers maintain some presence within Thyren, and many who have never travelled beyond its borders mistakenly assume it represents the entirety of the world.

Kareth occupies a position near the centre of the known world and has earned a reputation as a hub of commerce and trade. Goods from every corner of Nerra eventually find their way through Kareth's markets, making it one of the wealthiest regions known to modern scholars.

Caelyx remains a source of fascination and concern. The Shard's long association with the Rift has inspired countless theories regarding its history. Some claim the Cataclysm struck Caelyx harder than anywhere else. Others insist the Shard played a role in the disaster itself. Reliable evidence supporting either claim remains frustratingly scarce.

Morvane is perhaps the least understood of the major Shards. What little information reaches the wider world often comes from sailors, explorers and merchants whose stories rarely agree. Most descriptions depict a land still scarred by ancient devastation, though separating truth from embellishment has proven difficult.

Brassreach stands apart from the others. Renowned for its industry and technological sophistication, the Shard has developed a reputation for innovation that rivals even the greatest achievements of the former Dwarven Empire. Visitors often return with stories of machinery and inventions that seem decades ahead of anything found elsewhere in Nerra.

Despite their differences, the Shards share one common truth.

They are separated.

The Sunders ensure that no people, kingdom or empire can ever truly dominate the entire world. Every voyage between Shards remains a deliberate undertaking. Every trade route exists at significant risk. Every traveller who crosses the void is reminded that the world itself bears scars older than any living civilisation.

Perhaps that is why the Shards continue to capture the imagination.

They are not merely places.

They are fragments.

Remnants of a world that once existed and a constant reminder that no one alive truly knows what was lost when it broke.

---

_Archivist's Note: Magister Thorne spent nearly three decades compiling records from merchants, explorers, naval captains and scholarly institutions throughout Nerra. While generally considered reliable, critics argue that his work occasionally favours compelling narratives over hard evidence, particularly regarding the origins of the Shards._
