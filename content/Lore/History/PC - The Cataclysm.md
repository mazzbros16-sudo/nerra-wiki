
## At A Glance

The Cataclysm was the single most devastating event in recorded history and marks the dividing line between the ancient world and the modern age. Although its exact cause remains unknown, nearly every scholar, faith and civilisation agrees that the world was fundamentally changed by it.

The event is believed to have triggered widespread planar instability, reshaped much of Nerra, and coincided with the appearance of the Rift. Entire nations vanished, coastlines shifted, and countless historical records were lost. Many of the world's greatest mysteries can be traced back to this period.

The Cataclysm also preceded the formation of the Sunders, the vast voids that now separate the major landmasses of Nerra. Whether these fractures were a direct result of the disaster or merely one of its many consequences remains fiercely debated.

Despite centuries of study, no universally accepted explanation for the Cataclysm exists. To some, it was a divine tragedy. To others, an arcane disaster. Many believe the truth was lost alongside the world that existed before it.

## Historical Account

_Excerpt from **Fragments of a Broken World: A Study of the Cataclysm**, authored by Scholar-Archivist Theodric Vane of the Astral Collegium._

---

There is a question that has haunted every age since the world was broken.

What happened?

Entire libraries have been filled attempting to answer it. Kings have funded expeditions in search of forgotten truths. Priests have devoted lifetimes to prayer and contemplation. Scholars have argued, fought and occasionally killed one another in pursuit of answers.

Yet after centuries of investigation, the Cataclysm remains as elusive as ever.

What little can be stated with confidence is frightening enough.

Before the Cataclysm, the world appears to have been very different. Ancient maps depict lands no longer found on any modern chart. References survive to cities, kingdoms and peoples that seemingly vanished overnight. In some cases, entire cultures exist only as names preserved in damaged records.

Then, at some point in the distant past, everything changed.

The planes became unstable. Natural laws appear to have faltered. Vast regions were devastated or altered beyond recognition. Survivors recorded impossible storms, ruptures in reality, and phenomena that modern scholars struggle to explain. In many surviving accounts, there is a recurring sense of confusion. Those who lived through the disaster often seemed incapable of understanding what was unfolding around them.

Perhaps the most enduring legacy of the Cataclysm is the appearance of the Rift.

Whether the Rift caused the disaster, emerged because of it, or merely became visible during the chaos remains unknown. What is known is that references to the Rift become dramatically more common in historical records following the Cataclysm. Since then, it has remained one of the greatest sources of fear, fascination and scholarly inquiry within Nerra.

The Sunders also appear shortly after this period. Where once there may have been continuous land, now there are only impossible voids separating the world's great Shards. The exact process by which these fractures formed remains one of the most contested subjects in modern academia.

Religion offers no shortage of explanations.

Some faiths teach that the gods waged war against one another. Others claim the mortal world was punished for some forgotten transgression. Certain sects insist the Cataclysm was a necessary sacrifice that prevented an even greater disaster. Unsurprisingly, these claims rarely agree with one another.

The difficulty is that nearly every explanation suffers from the same flaw.

Evidence.

The Cataclysm destroyed so much that any investigation into its causes inevitably becomes an investigation into absence. Historians do not study what survived. They study what is missing.

Missing records.

Missing civilisations.

Missing truths.

Even today, expeditions occasionally uncover ruins that appear older than the Cataclysm itself. Such discoveries often create more questions than answers. Every fragment recovered seems only to reveal how much has been lost.

Perhaps that is why the Cataclysm continues to fascinate scholars.

Most historical mysteries concern events that happened.

The Cataclysm concerns a world that no longer exists.

And until the truth is discovered, every generation will continue asking the same question as the last.

What happened?

---

_Archivist's Note: Scholar-Archivist Vane's work is considered one of the most comprehensive modern studies of the Cataclysm. Critics have noted that his conclusions often favour uncertainty where stronger theories may exist. Vane himself famously responded to such criticism by writing, "Confidence and evidence are not the same thing."_
