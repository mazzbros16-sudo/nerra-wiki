## At A Glance

The Smoke War was the defining conflict of modern Aedryn and the event most responsible for shaping the continent that exists today. Fought between the Dwarven Empire and the peoples of Aedryn, the war ultimately ended centuries of dwarven expansion and occupation across much of the continent.

The conflict began after decades of growing dwarven influence, fuelled by technological superiority, industrial might, and a belief that dwarven civilisation represented the future of Aedryn. What began as expansion gradually transformed into conquest, bringing large portions of the continent beneath dwarven control.

Following the Night of Red Smoke and the rise of widespread resistance movements, humans, elves, ratfolk, orcs and liberated automatons united beneath the banner of the Velmoran Alliance. Years of brutal warfare eventually led to the collapse of the empire and the liberation of occupied territories.

Although the war ended decades ago, its legacy remains visible throughout Aedryn. Political tensions, distrust of authority, economic hardship, cultural division and lingering prejudice can all be traced, in one way or another, to the scars left behind by the Smoke War.

## Historical Account

_Excerpt from_ **_The Fall of Empire: A Study of the Smoke War_**_, authored by Professor Aldren Voss of the Astral Collegium, published 12 years after the conclusion of the conflict._

---

There are few events in modern history that have shaped Aedryn as profoundly as the Smoke War. Even now, decades after the final battles were fought, the conflict continues to influence politics, religion, trade, and the relationships between entire peoples.

To understand the war, one must first understand the world that existed before it.

Long before the modern era, the dwarves of the Stoneward Spine established themselves as the foremost industrial and military power upon the continent. While many nations pursued faith, magic, philosophy, or commerce, the dwarves devoted themselves almost entirely to engineering, production, and technological advancement. Their cities grew wealthy. Their armies became unmatched. Their influence spread steadily beyond the mountains.

What began as expansion eventually became conquest.

Many dwarven records from the period speak of a belief that their civilisation represented the natural future of Aedryn. Other cultures were often described as inefficient, unstable, or incapable of governing themselves properly. Whether this belief was born from arrogance, genuine conviction, or simple political necessity remains a matter of debate amongst scholars.

What is not debated is the result.

Within a generation, vast portions of the continent found themselves beneath dwarven occupation.

The event most commonly cited as the beginning of open rebellion is the tragedy now remembered as the Night of Red Smoke.

Unfortunately, reliable accounts remain scarce.

Velmoran records describe a peaceful protest that was violently suppressed by occupying forces. Surviving dwarven documents claim armed agitators provoked a military response. Independent accounts are frustratingly limited, often contradicting one another in significant ways.

Whatever the truth, the aftermath is far clearer.

Hundreds died.

Perhaps thousands.

The city of Karnhold became a symbol throughout the continent, and the names of those lost were carried far beyond its walls.

In the years that followed, resistance movements that had previously acted independently began to unite. Humans, elves, ratfolk, orcs, and eventually liberated automatons found common cause against a shared enemy. This alliance would become known as the Velmoran Alliance.

The war that followed was brutal.

Ratfolk operatives sabotaged supply lines and infrastructure hidden deep within dwarven territory. Orc tribes launched coordinated uprisings against those who had enslaved them. Automatons turned upon their former masters, bringing with them invaluable knowledge of dwarven logistics and military production.

Entire settlements vanished from maps.

Ancient forests burned.

Mountain strongholds collapsed.

The war consumed nearly every corner of Aedryn.

As the conflict dragged on, the empire began to fracture. Supply lines stretched thin. Internal divisions grew. Rumours persist of political betrayals within the highest levels of dwarven leadership, though many relevant records were lost during the final years of the conflict.

Eventually the empire collapsed.

The mountains were seized.

Former dwarven territories were divided amongst the victorious powers.

Peace returned to Aedryn.

At least officially.

The scars left by the Smoke War remain visible even today.

Many of the continent's governments were shaped by wartime fears. Distrust of authority remains widespread. Entire peoples continue to carry inherited grievances against one another. Even younger generations who never witnessed the conflict often speak of it as though it happened yesterday.

The war is over.

Most agree on that much.

Whether its consequences have truly ended is a question that Aedryn still struggles to answer.
