## At A Glance

The Night of Red Smoke was a pivotal event during the Dwarven occupation of Aedryn and is widely regarded as one of the turning points that led to the Smoke War.

The incident took place in the city of Karnhold, where a protest against Dwarven rule ended in widespread bloodshed. Exactly what occurred remains heavily debated. What is known is that Dwarven forces responded with overwhelming force, resulting in the deaths of countless civilians and resistance members.

Though the uprising itself failed, many historians regard the Night of Red Smoke as the spark that transformed scattered resentment into organised rebellion across the continent.

## Historical Account

_Excerpt from **The Fall of Empire: A Study of the Smoke War**, authored by Professor Aldren Voss of the Astral Collegium._

---

No event from the years preceding the Smoke War has been studied, debated or politicised more heavily than the Night of Red Smoke.

Even now, decades later, agreement remains elusive.

What can be stated with certainty is remarkably little.

The city of Karnhold had endured Dwarven occupation for many years. Tensions within the city had steadily worsened as resistance movements gained support amongst the local population. By the time of the incident, public frustration with imperial rule had become impossible to ignore.

On the night in question, a large gathering formed within the city.

Why they gathered depends largely upon which source one consults.

Velmoran accounts describe a peaceful demonstration intended to protest occupation and inspire wider resistance. Surviving Dwarven records speak of organised agitators preparing for insurrection. Several independent accounts suggest the truth may have lain somewhere between the two.

What happened next remains the subject of fierce disagreement.

At some point, violence erupted.

Whether this violence originated from protestors, resistance fighters, Dwarven soldiers or some combination thereof has never been conclusively established. Witness testimonies frequently contradict one another, and many records from the period were destroyed during the war.

The response, however, is beyond dispute.

Dwarven forces moved to suppress the gathering.

The operation escalated rapidly.

By dawn, large sections of Karnhold had been reduced to ruins, smoke hung over the city, and the streets were littered with the dead.

No reliable casualty figures exist. Some estimates number the dead in the hundreds. Others place the toll far higher.

In the immediate aftermath, the rebellion had failed. Its leaders were dead, imprisoned or scattered. Order had been restored. Yet history rarely concerns itself with immediate outcomes.

Across Aedryn, stories of Karnhold spread faster than soldiers ever could. Whether entirely accurate or not, they carried a powerful message. The occupation could be resisted. More importantly, many believed it must be.

The rebellion had been extinguished. The idea behind it had not. For this reason, many historians identify the Night of Red Smoke not as the beginning of the Smoke War, but as the moment its arrival became inevitable.
