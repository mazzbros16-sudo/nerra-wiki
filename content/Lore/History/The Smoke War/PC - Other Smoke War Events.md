## Other Notable Events

While the Night of Red Smoke is often viewed as the spark that ignited the Smoke War, several other events proved equally important in the eventual collapse of the Dwarven Empire.

### The Ratfolk Betrayal

For generations, Ratfolk lived within the shadows of the Dwarven Holds. Though largely ignored by the Empire, they possessed an intimate understanding of its tunnels, infrastructure and supply routes. When the Ratfolk openly pledged themselves to the Velmoran Alliance, they provided information that allowed Alliance forces to penetrate strongholds previously considered impregnable. Many historians consider their involvement one of the decisive factors in the fall of the Stoneward Spine.

### The Automaton Uprisings

Originally created by the dwarves as labourers and soldiers, automatons served throughout the Empire for generations. During the war, increasing numbers began turning against their creators and joining the Velmoran Alliance. Their knowledge of Dwarven logistics, production facilities and military operations proved invaluable, while their rebellion became a symbol of liberation across the continent.

### The Orc Rebellions

Unlike the automatons, the orcs required no liberation before rising against the Empire. Having endured generations of subjugation following their defeat in the mountains, many tribes launched their own coordinated uprisings during the war. These rebellions would eventually align with the Velmoran Alliance, creating a common front against Dwarven rule and stretching imperial resources across multiple fronts.

### The Political Fractures

Many historians believe the Empire's enemies were not solely responsible for its collapse. Persistent rumours speak of political rivalries, betrayals and internal divisions within the Dwarven leadership during the final years of the war. While evidence remains scarce, few scholars dispute that the Empire was struggling to maintain unity long before its final defeat.

### The Fall of the Empire

Ultimately, the combined pressure of rebellion, invasion, overextended supply lines and internal instability proved too much to withstand. The Dwarven Empire collapsed, its territories divided amongst the victors and its people driven from much of the Stoneward Spine.
