## At A Glance

The Velmoran Alliance was the coalition formed to oppose the Dwarven Empire during the Smoke War. It united humans, elves, ratfolk, orcs, automatons and countless smaller groups beneath a common cause for the first time in Aedryn's history.

Though tensions and disagreements existed between its members, the Alliance transformed scattered resistance into an organised military force capable of challenging dwarven rule. Many historians regard its formation as the true beginning of the Smoke War.

The war that followed lasted for many years and left deep scars across the continent. Despite the devastation, the Alliance ultimately emerged victorious, bringing an end to the Dwarven Empire's control of Aedryn.

## Historical Account

_Excerpt from **The Rise of the Alliance**, preserved within the archives of Velor._

---

History often remembers victories as inevitable.

The formation of the Velmoran Alliance was anything but.

For generations, the peoples of Aedryn had lived alongside one another without truly standing together. Old grudges lingered. Borders divided communities. Trade disputes, cultural differences and historical rivalries ensured that cooperation rarely extended beyond necessity.

The Dwarven Empire understood this well.

For many years, resistance movements arose throughout the continent. Some fought openly. Others operated from the shadows. Most were defeated in isolation.

The Night of Red Smoke changed that.

News of the massacre at Karnhold spread across Aedryn with a speed that surprised even those who carried the stories. Whether through outrage, fear or simple exhaustion, many began to realise that resistance would continue to fail so long as each people fought alone.

Slowly, alliances began to form.

Human settlements pledged soldiers and supplies. Ellei communities opened communication with neighbouring groups they had long ignored. Ratfolk networks carried information between resistance cells. Orc tribes rose against former masters. Even automatons, once created to serve the empire, began turning against it.

What emerged was unlike anything the continent had seen before.

Not a kingdom.

Not an empire.

An alliance.

The early years were difficult. Resources were scarce. Trust was limited. Victory was far from certain. Yet despite these challenges, the coalition endured.

As the war dragged on, so too did the Alliance grow.

Every victory inspired another uprising. Every setback strengthened the resolve of those who remained. Entire regions joined the cause, until what had once been a collection of isolated rebellions became a continent-wide movement.

The cost was staggering.

Cities burned.

Forests were scarred.

Generations were lost to conflict.

Yet by the war's end, the impossible had become reality.

The Dwarven Empire had fallen.

Many of the nations that exist today trace their origins to agreements first forged within the Alliance. While the coalition itself would eventually dissolve, its legacy remains visible throughout Aedryn.

For a brief moment in history, peoples who had every reason to remain divided chose a different path.

And together, they changed the world.
